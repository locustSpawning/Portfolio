South Jersey Rescue

For this project, my goal was to do a total re-design of an existing business while creating a fully dynamic front-end demo of what the website would be if launched. I conducted research and usability testing to gain insights on what users would need from a service that would be collecting payments.

My role and responsibilites

For this project I designed and developed the front end of this website. I was responsible for research, interviews, design, and development.

For this project, I collaborated with my mentor who is a back-end developer. My goal was to improve on the existing design while gaining an understanding of the needs and expectations of the back-end. While this website did not launch to the public, it was a truly valuable learning experience that showcases significant improvement on information architecture, accessibility, and branding design.

This project was for demo purposes only, and South Jersey Rescue owns all rights to their images, website, and brand identity.

The most significant problem I addressed when observing SJ Rescue’s website was their lack of information architecture. The website was utterly disorganized and confusing in terms of navigation, so I began with paper wireframes to gain an understanding of how to best organize the information on SJ Rescue’s website.

Next, I designed low fidelity prototypes using Figma to map out how these wire-frames might look on a digital screen.

Now, I was ready to create the high fidelity prototypes. These were designed for both desktop and mobile screens. For a more detailed look, here is the link to my Figma file.
